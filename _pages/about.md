*My research interests include power-management ICs (PMICs)—particularly high-voltage-conversion-ratio DC–DC converters and highly integrated on-chip and in-package voltage regulators (IVRs)—with an emphasis on high current density, high efficiency, fast transient response, and easy scalability.*

---

## Research Highlights

**① Scalable Distributed Converter with One-Pin Current Balancing** — A 12V-to-1V four-phase array achieving 22 A output at 89.1% peak efficiency, using a single shared bus for O(1) current self-balancing across distributed units, directly targeting Power Chiplet architectures. *(CICC 2026)*

**② Dual-Loop Fast-Slow Hybrid Converter** — A single-chip dual-module design coupling a high-efficiency slow path (~1 MHz) with a high-bandwidth fast path (~20 MHz), reducing voltage undershoot by 12.2× and overshoot by 12.4× while maintaining co-stable dual-loop operation. *(ISSCC 2025, Highlight Paper)*

**③ Fast-Slow Two-Module DC-DC Solution** — Decouples steady-state efficiency from transient performance by adding a small auxiliary module for high-di/dt injection, cutting undershoot from 115 mV to 13 mV (8.8×) and recovery from 80 µs to 2.5 µs (32×). *(CICC 2024 / JSSC 2025)*

**④ Inductor-First Inductor-on-Ground Hybrid Converter** — Repositions the inductor upstream to achieve quasi-continuous input current, reducing EMI and conduction loss. Covers 3–5 V input (Li-ion / USB), achieving 96.1% peak efficiency and 1.02 A/mm² current density. *(CICC 2023 / JSSC 2024)*

**⑤ Symmetrical Double Step-Down Converter** — Breaks the conventional D < 0.5 duty-cycle constraint in hybrid converters, extending the usable voltage conversion ratio across the full Li-ion discharge range. *(TCAS-I 2022, 65nm CMOS)*
